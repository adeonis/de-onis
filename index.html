The Story So Far

The de Onís family traces its roots to diplomacy and vision. Beginning in the early 19th century with Luis de Onís, a Spanish diplomat of remarkable acumen, the family became known for bridging continents and cultures. Through treaty negotiations and political service, they established themselves as a bridge between the Old World and the New — between Spain and the Americas, between policy and principle.

Across subsequent generations, the family's defining trait remained constant: a commitment to understanding, preserving, and communicating across boundaries. Whether through scholarship, journalism, or filmmaking, each generation found new mediums for the same essential work — telling stories that illuminate the human condition and fostering deeper connections across difference.

Today, the de Onís family continues this legacy through diverse endeavors in media, culture, and social justice. This website honors that continuity — not as a static archive, but as a living document of how one family's commitment to bridging worlds manifests across centuries and disciplines.
